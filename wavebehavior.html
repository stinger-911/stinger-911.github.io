Reflection

Reflection is when a wave changes direction by bouncing off a material. Mirrors are a good example of reflection, light bounces off the glass and projects an image, but another good example is echos. An echo is when sound waves are reflected off an object, the direction changes but the sound is still the same.

Refraction

Refraction is when a wave changes direction because it goes through a new medium. Rainbows are created by light refracting off of rain or mist, and as it leaves, each color comes out at different angles (since they have different speeds) and create a rainbow. Refraction is even used in glasses, the lenses bend light to allow you see more clearly.

Diffraction

Diffraction is when a wave changes direction by passing through an opening or going around a barrier, once its through, the wave will spread out. You can see diffraction when light is trying to go out of a semi-closed door. The light goes around the door, and out, but as it is going out, the light spreads out. You can also see it in the clouds, light will go around the clouds and pass through, then it spreads out.


Interference

Interference occurs when two or more waves are in the same place at the same time. There is destructive interference and constructive interference.
Constructive Interference is when two interfering waves are in a phase and make a larger wave. This means that the troughs and crests of one wave will align with the troughs and crests of the second wave. When you add the two waves, they make an even bigger wave. If you put two speakers next to each other, sometimes they'll make a larger noise, this is constructive interference.

Dustructive Interference is when the two interfering waves are out of phase with each other and make a smaller wave. The troughs of one wave will align with the crests of the other and vice-versa, they will be added, but instead of getting bigger, the two waves will essentially cancel each other out. Noise cancelling headphones use destructive interference so that you can hear only your music. It picks up the sound waves around you with a microphone on the outside and produces a sound wave that will cancel it out which allows you to listen to your music without outside interruptions.



Resonance

Resonance happens when an object that is vibrating causes another object with the same natural frequency to vibrate with it. Natural frequency is the frequency an object produces when struck. Resonance will not work if the two objects do not have the same natural frequency. If you hit a tuning fork near another tuning fork with the same natural frequency, the one that wasn't hit will also vibrate because of resonance. You can also break a wine glass with resonance, as long as the sound you make has the same natural frequency of the glass, the glass will begin to vibrate, and eventually it will break.



The Doppler Effect

The Doppler Effect is the increase or decrease in the frequency of a wave as the source of the sound and observer either move away from each other or towards each other. As the object making the wave gets closer to you, the wavelength of the wave gets shorter and the frequency gets higher. As the object moves away from you, the wavelength gets longer and the frequency gets lower. This causes the chanage in pitch when a car passes you, as it gets closer, the sound gets louder, and once it passes you, the sound gets quieter. It is also used in astronomy to see if two galaxies are moving towards or away from each other, the closer, the more blue a redshift will be, the further apart, the more red.
